As For Me and My House

Bible verse about choosing to serve the Lord

Understanding "As For Me and My House"

The phrase "as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord" comes from Joshua 24:15, one of the most powerful declarations of faith in the Bible. This verse represents a personal and family commitment to serve God, regardless of what others choose to do. It's a statement of resolve and dedication that has inspired countless families throughout history.

"But if serving the Lord seems undesirable to you, then choose for yourselves this day whom you will serve, whether the gods your ancestors served beyond the Euphrates, or the gods of the Amorites, in whose land you are living. But as for me and my household, we will serve the Lord."
Joshua 24:15 (NIV)

The Context of Joshua 24:15

This verse comes at the end of Joshua's life, as he addresses the people of Israel. After leading them into the Promised Land and witnessing God's faithfulness, Joshua challenges the people to make a choice: will they serve the Lord or turn to other gods? He then makes his own bold declaration: "as for me and my household, we will serve the Lord."

The Meaning of "As For Me and My House"

This declaration represents several important principles:

Personal Choice: Joshua makes a personal decision to serve the Lord, regardless of what others choose.

Family Commitment: He extends this commitment to his entire household, showing the importance of leading one's family in faith.

Resolve and Determination: The statement is definitive and unwavering, showing Joshua's complete dedication to God.

Leadership by Example: Joshua leads by example, showing others what it means to choose God.

Applying This Verse Today

"As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord" is a powerful statement that families can make today. It means:

Making God a Priority: Putting God first in our personal lives and family decisions.

Leading Our Families: Taking responsibility for the spiritual direction of our households.

Standing Firm: Remaining committed to God even when others choose differently.

Serving Together: Working as a family to serve God and others.

The Choice to Serve

Joshua's statement reminds us that serving God is a choice we must make daily. It's not something that happens automatically, but requires intentional decision and commitment. When we choose to serve the Lord, we align our lives with His purposes and experience the blessings that come from faithful service.

Conclusion

"As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord" is more than a popular Bible verse—it's a declaration of faith, commitment, and leadership. It challenges us to make a choice, to lead our families in faith, and to remain steadfast in our service to God. May we, like Joshua, have the courage to declare our commitment to serve the Lord, both individually and as families, regardless of what others choose.
